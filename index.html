Using data analytics to combat stray animal populations!

Furever Finders

We have all seen a stray animal. Have you ever wondered what happens when they are picked up by an organization? We wanted to better understand stray animal figures and see what happened to those found throughout the city. It is through better understanding of the process and sharing of this information where we felt we could make an impact on the stray animal population in Houston. The first problem we encountered was not being able to find free information here in Houston. Our research led us to readily accessible information provided by the city of Austin. Below is data gathered by the City of Austin during their stray animal intake. Our initial findings dated back to 2013 and comprised of over 100,000 animals having gone through the intake process. The average age of a cat in the shelter is 1 year old while a dog is 2 years old. There are more dogs in the shelter averaging about 56% to 43% cats. An interesting finding was that no cat over 5 years old was reported in the shelter.

Live Animal Intake Tracking

Utilizing real time data you are now able to locate any animal within Austin and easily determine their availability. Please note that an image is only available when an animal is ready to be adopted. We see this tool as one of the most powerful and easy to use features that could potentially impact your every day consumers to stimulate pet adoptions. Convenience and ease of use is key when presenting a problem. By allowing someone to readily zoom in and out near their home and have their picture populate would help expedite pet adoptions.

Intake Historical Data

Use the table below to find information on what happened to an animal. You can search within any field. Currently, within these 46,000+ unique animal entries the breakdown is as follows: 48% were adopted, 30% transfered, 18% returned to owners and less than 2% euthanized.

In conclusion, we feel the city of Houston should provide developers and the general public, with the information already being collected during intake to help tackle the stray animal population problem. In the age of modern technology, any little bit can help.
